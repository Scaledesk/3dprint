3D printing in classroom!

3D printing is a low-cost approach to learning perfectly suited for classrooms.

Children love to explore and try new things, but many are afraid to try, they are scared of failure.
3D printing removes that fear, instead it embodies them with confidence to venture into the unknown.

Schools that have adopted 3D printing are seeing amazing results among students. The creative process involved in planning and printing 3D objects motivates students to achieve further success.

Whether it be solving algebraic puzzles or designing products, the ways 3D printing can be used in the classroom are numerous.

GOOD REASONS FOR SCHOOLS TO INVEST IN 3D PRINTING

In the near future 3D printing is going to be used on a global scale – our pupils need to start getting comfortable with it now.

It's hard to beat the ‘WOW’ factor that a 3D printer gives you in a lesson.

It helps to translate abstract maths problems to the physical world.

3D printing is a great fit for the STEM curriculum and other subjects.

The 'price barrier' for schools is lower than ever

Printing out prototypes can help students refine their designs and better understand the creation process.
